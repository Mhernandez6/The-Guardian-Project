What is Domestic Violence

Domestic Violence can pretty much happen to anyone who is in a relationship or even including their own family members, it doesn’t discriminate against everyone. Females are the ones who are often victims of abuse themselves, while males also get abused themselves. The bottom line is that no one else deserves to be abused by others; everyone should feel safe and respected in the environment.

Types of Abuse

Emotional abuse includes verbal abuse such as yelling, name-calling, blaming, and shaming. Isolation, intimidation, and controlling behavior are also forms of emotional abuse.

● Threatens to tell friends, family, colleagues or community members your sexual orientation or gender identity

● Calls you names, insults you or puts you down

● Tries to control how you spend money, where you go, what medicines you take or what you wear

● Prevents or discourages you from going to work or school or seeing family members or friends

What can you do if you are a victim of domestic Violence

● Leave When It Is Safe To Do So

● Avoid Trying To Dismiss The Abuse

● Avoid Falling For The “Nice Guy/Girl Act” After An Abusive Episode

● Give Yourself Some Credit!
